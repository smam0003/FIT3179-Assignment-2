Paralympics

Background

The Paralympic Games is a quadrennial event that allows para-athletes with ranging disabilities and impairment levels to compete in many different international sports. Usually held shortly after and in the same city as the Olympics, the Paralympics aims to provide the same worldwide exposure for para-athletes as those who are able-bodied. This visualisation aims to provide various analyses on the Games in terms of finding out the most para-athlete producing countries, most successful continent, athletesâ€™ successes, and team balance.

Which Country Produces The Most Paralympians?

The worldwide spread of Paralympics has increased tremendously since its inception, with over 162 participating countries in the 2020 Tokyo Paralympics compared to only 17 countries in the 1960 Rome Games. More and more countries from Africa, Asia, and South America have participated in the Games, where there were barely any in the beginning. Looking into Australia, it has had consistent number of athletes per 10 million population, ranging from about 10.7 to 149.32. The highest consistent Paralympian-producing country is Iceland holding over a record 560.68 athletes per 10 million population in 1988. There is a trend of countries producing more para-athletes the more away the countries are located from the Equator due to the higher advocacy for the Paralympic Games amongst those places to encourage more athletes to participate.



Athletes' Successes: Does Correlation Between Player And Medal Numbers Exist?

According to the chart, there is indeed a positive medium correlation between the number of participating athletes and medals won in all types which suggest that the higher the number of athletes, the more medals the country is going to win. One notable feature is that there are some countries with a very high medal to athlete ratio, (i.e China, 2020 (0.81) suggesting 81% of the athletes come home with a medal). This suggests that the closer to the top-right of the plot the country is, the higher the medal-to-athlete ratio. Thus countries that aim to have more success should focus on increasing their athlete's attendance.



Battle Of The Continents: Which Continent Was The Most Successful?

(Normalised By Number of Countries Attended Each Continent)

For many years, North American countries have dominated the Games, ranking first 8 times from 1960-1988 in total medals, but went on a decline since then. The top rank is replaced by Oceanian countries, partly because of their rise in medals won while having a small number of countries. The most consistent continent is Asia, ranking fourth consistently throughout. Another notable finding is the massive drop in ranking for Europe in '64 and '68 due to some of its countries having poor performance. There are similar changes in rankings for gold and silver medals, but significant fluctuations in brown medals for second between Europe and NA, and fifth between Africa and SA due to the extra competition in the last place on the podium.



Which Continent Had The Most Balanced Team?

Unfortunately, the Paralympics Games had been the majority male-dominant event. However, in the 2020 Games, North American countries combined has achieved the lowest % difference between men and women at about 1%, with 246 men and 243 women athletes. It is only a matter of time in the future where we will see more representation of women para-athletes in the event, balancing both men and women athletes. The overall trend worldwide looks to decline in the % difference between male and female athletes as well, suggesting more exposure to female athletes to participate.
